ANALYSIS

Month of year hotspots and coldspots, and what to expect during August 2023

For the crime of larceny in the City of Fairfax, the three months with that contained days with the highest number of calls were July, December, and January. July and December showed the highest number of calls from 2009 to 2011. In 2023, January had the high number of calls. February showed to be the month with the continuous least number of calls throughout the time period. So far in 2023, January through April have all experienced an upward trend in larceny calls. August 2023 is expected to follow the trend and either stay relatively the same or show an increase of larceny calls.

Day of week hotspots and coldspots, and what to expect next week

The coldest days are Saturday and Sunday. The hottest days for larcenies are Monday through Friday between 9 am and 5 pm. The hottest times for larcenies are during the normal work week. This suggests larcenies are a crime of opportunity when most people come to the city for work. All the evidence suggests that Monday through Friday of next week between 9 am and 5 pm will experience a high number of calls, with most occurring on Monday and Friday.

Hour of day hotspots, coldspots, trends, and patterns

As referenced in the previous paragraph, 9 am to 5 pm Monday through Friday are hotspots. Calls for larceny start tapering off at 6 pm. The hottest time on the model is Friday at 4 pm. The coldspots are between 11 pm and 7 am, with the coldest time at 4 am. Saturday, Sunday, and Friday to a smaller extent, show increased activity between 1 am and 3 am compared to the other days of the week. The coldest coldest time on the model is 4 am on every day except Sunday.
